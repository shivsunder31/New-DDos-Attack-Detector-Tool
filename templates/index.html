What is DDoS?

A distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) attack is a malicious attempt to disrupt the normal traffic of a targeted server, service or network by overwhelming the target or its surrounding infrastructure with a flood of InternetÂ traffic.

"Amateurs hack system;
professionals hack people."
-Bruce Schneier

DoS (Denial of Service): A DoS attack employs a single source to flood a target with excessive traffic, aiming to exhaust
resources and render it inaccessible.

DDoS (Distributed Denial of Service): DDoS involves a coordinated attack from multiple sources (botnet), overwhelming a
target with a massive influx of traffic for heightened disruption.

how Hackers
do

Cyber adversaries orchestrate a disruption in online services by implementing techniques that generate a substantial volume of spurious requests or network traffic, thereby inducing a state of congestion or overload commonly referred to as a "traffic jam" on the targeted system or network.
